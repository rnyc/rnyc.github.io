A Week with Pluckeye Android

Pluckeye is a software utility developed by Single Eye Software LLC in Oregon. It was created to help human beings who lack perfect self-control. It began as software for traditional computers and it works by giving you tools to limit what and when you consume the internet.

With the explosion of mobile devices, Singe Eye Software made a foray into the Android market by developing a browser that it does not show any media existing on a web page - no pictures, videos, sounds. Why? Because some people enage in online activities they know to be detrimental, but lack the tools and self-control to get the help they need.

But while Pluckeye can do a good job by itself on a traditional computer, the Android architecture needs Pluckeye + something to make a complete solution.

This page is my report on using the Pluckeye Browser on an android device over the course of a week in April 2018.

Step 1: Developing a Scenario

In my scenario I am a person with online self-control issues. I want to access the internet but my needs are primarily textual information (news sites). I want to access some limited online areas with full access, and hope to do that through dedicated apps. 

I have a trusted friend who (my wife) who is going to lock my tablet down. When I need changes made, she will be the one to unlock it.

(What if I don't have a trusted friend nearby?)

Step 2: Setup

The first thing was to get the hardware ready.

  1. I used a Sony Iconium tablet that I borrowed from my employer.
  2. I installed the Pluckeye Browser.
  3. I installed a subscription magazine app I use for testing purposes.
  4. I installed a web-based educational app for testing purposes.
  5. I installed the Perfect KeyLock App. [Why choose this?]

Then I configured Perfect KeyLock App.

  1. You need to give it permission to sees what you are doing.
  2. You need to let the device be and administrator to prevent you from force quitting it.
  3. You can then lock every app that you don't want to use to access the Internet.
